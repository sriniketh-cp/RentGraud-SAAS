Why Fresh Paint Doesn't Protect You

In Indian real estate, the standard rental agreement often includes a "Painting Clause", stating the tenant must pay one month's rent for repainting upon vacating. However, this creates a false sense of security.

The Hidden Danger of "Wear and Tear"

While fresh paint covers scuff marks, it does not repair structural issues. Landlords frequently deduct heavy amounts from security deposits for issues that paint cannot fix, such as:

  • Cracked marble flooring or broken bathroom tiles.
  • Pre-existing internal water seepage (dampness).
  • Faulty electrical fittings or loose door hardware.
"RentGuard establishes a baseline. By proving a defect existed on Day 1, you protect yourself from paying for damages you didn't cause, regardless of the painting clause."

By using RentGuard to take extreme close-ups of these specific pre-existing damages with text annotations and digital signatures, you generate a legal standing that standard property listing photos simply cannot provide.
